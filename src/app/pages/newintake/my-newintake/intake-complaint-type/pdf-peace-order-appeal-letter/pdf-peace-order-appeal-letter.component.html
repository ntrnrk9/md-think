Intake Process for Peace Order Complaints

The purpose of the Intake Interview is to determine whether the Juvenile Court has jurisdiction over the complaint and, if so, whether judicial action is in the best interest of the public, the youth or both.

After determining that the court has jurisdiction, the Intake Officer must make one of the following decisions within 25 days of the date that the complaint is received by the Department of Juvenile Services.

  1. Close the complaint at intake with a warning;
  2. With the consent of the victim, the youth and the youth’s parent(s)/guardian(s)/custodian(s), place the youth on Informal Supervision for up to 90 days, unless otherwise extended by the Court; or
  3. Authorize the filing of the Peace Order application with a referral to the Juvenile Court Clerk for scheduling of a Court Hearing.

At the Intake Interview, the juvenile who is the subject of the complaint may have an attorney present. If the juvenile wishes to have an attorney present, one must be retained by the juvenile. At any point in the Intake Interview, the juvenile has the right to remain silent.

If the Intake Officer authorizes the filing of the Peace Order Complaint, any information received by the Intake Officer during the intake process cannot be used against the youth at the hearing to determine whether the youth committed the alleged delinquent act.

The complainant may appeal the Denial of authorization to file a Peace Order Complaint, to the Department of Juvenile Services’ Regional Director within 15 days of issuance of the denial.

If an application for a Peace Order is filed and the Court makes a finding that the act did, in fact occur the Court may place the youth under supervision and impose conditions that the Court, or any agency designated by the Court, deems appropriate.

If you do not understand any part of the information contained in this form, the Intake Officer will explain it to you at the time of the Intake Interview.
